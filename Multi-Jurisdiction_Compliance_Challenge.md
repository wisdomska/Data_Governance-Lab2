# Executive Summary

ShopGhana received three simultaneous data deletion requests from customers located in Ghana, Germany, and California. Each request triggers different legal rights, timelines, and operational obligations under the applicable data protection laws.

My analysis determined the following:

· Abena (Ghana) – Ghana’s Data Protection Act does not provide a full “right to be forgotten” equivalent, but individuals can request correction, blocking, or deletion of unnecessary data. ShopGhana may delete most account data but must retain financial records for statutory obligations.

· Lukas (Germany) – Under GDPR Article 17, Lukas has a clear legal right to erasure. ShopGhana must erase his personal data unless a lawful exemption applies (e.g., accounting/legal obligations). Response deadline: 1 month.

· Maria (California) – Under CCPA/CPRA, Maria has the right to deletion and the right to opt-out of sale/sharing of personal information. However, the active return dispute allows ShopGhana to temporarily retain data required to resolve the transaction.

The compliance response must ensure timely acknowledgment, lawful retention where required, and proper documentation of actions taken.


# Customer-Specific Legal Analysis

Here is an analysis of the various customers against ShopGhana’s obligations:

## Customer 1 – Abena (Ghana)

The applicable law under this scenario is the **Ghana Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843)**

### 1. Does Abena Have a Legal Right to Deletion?

Yes, but conditionally. The Ghana DPA allows individuals to request:

· Correction of inaccurate data

· Blocking or erasure of data that is unnecessary or unlawfully processed

However, it does not establish an explicit absolute for the right to be forgotten – therefore, deletion must be evaluated against data retention obligations.

### 2. ShopGhana's Obligations

ShopGhana must:

· Verify Abena’s identity.

· Assess whether the data is still necessary for the purpose it was collected.

· Delete or anonymize unnecessary personal data.

· Retain data required for legal or financial obligations.

### 3. Data That Can Be Retained

ShopGhana may retain:
| Data Type | Reason | Retention Duration |
|-----------|--------|--------------------|
| Transaction records | Tax and accounting compliance | 5–7 years |
| Fraud monitoring logs | Security obligations | 2–5 years |
| Legal compliance records | Regulatory audit requirements | As required by law |


### 1. Company’s Response Deadline

The Ghana DPA does not specify a strict statutory response timeframe, but best practice and regulator guidance recommend responding within 21 days.

### 2. Draft Response:

**_“Subject: Your Account Deletion Request – ShopGhana_**

_Dear Abena,_

_Thank you for contacting ShopGhana regarding the deletion of your account._

_We have verified your request and will proceed to deactivate and delete your account profile, marketing preferences, and associated browsing history._

_Certain limited transaction records related to your past purchases will be retained for statutory financial and regulatory compliance purposes as required under Ghanaian law. These records will not be used for marketing or any commercial purpose._

_Your account will be fully deactivated within 7 business days._

_If you have any additional questions about how your personal information is handled, please contact our Data Protection Officer._

_Kind regards,  
ShopGhana Data Protection Team”_

## Customer 2 – Lukas (Germany)

The applicable law under this scenario is the **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), Article 17 – Right to Erasure.**

### 1. Does Lukas Have a Legal Right to Erasure?

Yes, under GPDR Article 17, individuals may request deletion when:

· Data is no longer necessary

· Consent is withdrawn

· Processing is unlawful

· The subject of data objects to processing

Since Lukas has no outstanding orders or disputes, the right applies.

### 2. Exemptions That May Apply:

ShopGhana may retain data if necessary for:

· Legal obligations (tax and accounting laws)

· Defense of legal claims

· Fraud prevention

Thus, transaction invoices may still be retained.

### 3. Steps ShopGhana Must Take:

ShopGhana must:

· Verify Lukas' identity.

· Identify all personal data stored across systems.

· Delete or anonymize non-required personal data.

· Inform third-party processors of the deletion request.

· Document compliance action.

The systems affected by these actions may include the CRM, Marketing Tools, Payment Processors, Shipping Partners and Customer Analytics Databases.

### 4. Company’s Response Deadline

**1 month (30 days)** under GDPR, which may be extended by 2 additional months for complex requests, but the user must be notified.

### 5. Consequences of Missing the Deadline

If ShopGhana misses the response deadline, they might face:

· A potential penalty of up to €20 million or 4% of global annual revenue

· Complaints to EU Data Protection Authorities

· A mandatory regulatory investigation

### 3. Draft Response:

**_“Subject: Confirmation of Your GDPR Data Erasure Request_**

_Dear Lukas,_

_We have received your request to erase your personal data under Article 17 of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)._

_ShopGhana will delete your personal data associated with your account, including profile information, browsing history, and marketing data._

_Certain transaction records relating to completed purchases may be retained for the minimum period required under financial and tax regulations. These records will be securely restricted and will not be used for marketing purposes._

_We will complete the erasure process within 30 days and notify relevant service providers who process your data on our behalf._

_If you have further questions regarding your rights under GDPR, please contact our Data Protection Officer._

_Kind regards,_

_ShopGhana Data Protection Team_”

## Customer 3 – Maria (California)

The applicable law under this scenario is the **California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) / California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA)**

### 1. Maria’s Rights

Under CCPA/CPRA, Maria has the following rights:

· Right to delete personal information

· Right to opt out of the sale or sharing of personal data

· Right to know what data is collected

· Right to non-discrimination

### 2. Can ShopGhana Process Deletion Immediately?

No, because Maria has an active return dispute, the law allows businesses to retain personal information necessary to complete the transaction or resolve disputes. Therefore, account deletion must be temporarily paused for data necessary for the dispute to be retained.

### 3. Company’s Response to “Stop Selling” Request

With Processing occurring within 15 days under CPRA guidance, ShopGhana must:

· Immediately honor the Do Not Sell or Share request

· Update internal privacy flags

· Notify advertising partners and data brokers

### 4. Company’s Response Deadline

CCPA requires response within 45 days, extendable by another 45 days if necessary.

### 5. Required Disclosure to Maria

ShopGhana must inform Maria about:

· Categories of personal data retained

· Purpose of retention

· Estimated timeline for deletion after dispute resolution

· Confirmation of opt-out implementation

### 6. Draft Response:

**_“Subject: Response to Your Data Deletion and Privacy Request_**

_Dear Maria,_

_Thank you for contacting ShopGhana regarding your privacy request._

_We have successfully processed your request to stop the sale or sharing of your personal information with third parties. Your account has been flagged to ensure that your data is not used for advertising or data-sharing purposes._

_Regarding your request to delete your account, we identified an active return dispute related to a recent purchase. Under applicable California privacy laws, we are permitted to retain personal information necessary to complete transactions and resolve disputes._

_Once the dispute process has been concluded, we will proceed with deleting your personal information except for records required for legal or accounting purposes._

_We will provide confirmation once the deletion process has been completed._

_If you have any questions regarding your rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), please contact our Privacy Team._

_Kind regards,  
ShopGhana Privacy Office_”

# Practical Implementation Challenges Identified

Here are some key operational challenges in implementing multi-jurisdiction data deletion requests:

## 1. Cross-Border Compliance Complexity

ShopGhana must maintain **jurisdiction-aware privacy workflows** because each law has different obligations.

## 2. Data Mapping Challenges

Deletion requests require knowledge of all locations where personal data exists, including:

· marketing platforms

· payment providers

· shipping partners

· analytics databases

Without a data inventory and lineage, compliance becomes difficult.

## 3. Third-Party Processor Coordination

GDPR requires controllers to notify all processors holding the user’s data. Failure to do so creates compliance gaps.

## 4. Retention Policy Conflicts

Deletion rights often conflict with:

· tax retention laws

· fraud detection

· dispute resolution

A clear retention schedule is essential.
