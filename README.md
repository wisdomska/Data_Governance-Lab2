# Overview

This lab analyzes how **ShopGhana**, a global e-commerce platform selling African fashion and crafts, must handle **data deletion requests from customers across different legal jurisdictions**.

The exercise evaluates compliance obligations under three major data protection regulations:

- **Ghana Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843)**
- **European Union General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)**
- **California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) / California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA)**

Three customers from different regions submitted **data deletion requests on the same day**, requiring the company to determine the correct legal and operational response for each jurisdiction.

# Key Compliance Findings

## Ghana – Data Protection Act (DPA)

- The Ghana DPA allows **conditional deletion or blocking of personal data**.
- The law does **not explicitly provide a full "right to be forgotten"**.
- Companies may retain data required for:
  - Financial reporting
  - Legal compliance
  - Fraud monitoring

**Recommended response timeline:** ~21 days (best practice).

---

## European Union – GDPR

Under **GDPR Article 17 (Right to Erasure)**:

Customers have the right to request deletion when:

- Data is no longer necessary
- Consent is withdrawn
- Processing is unlawful
- The data subject objects to processing

However, companies may retain data for:

- Tax compliance
- Legal claims
- Fraud prevention

**Response deadline:** 30 days.

**Maximum penalty:**  
Up to **€20 million or 4% of global annual revenue**.

---

## California – CCPA / CPRA

Consumers have rights to:

- Delete personal information
- Opt-out of selling or sharing personal data
- Know what personal data is collected
- Non-discriminatory service

However, deletion may be **temporarily denied** if data is required to:

- Complete transactions
- Resolve disputes
- Detect security incidents

**Response deadline:** 45 days.

# Compliance Response Matrix

| Element | Ghana DPA | GDPR | CCPA / CPRA |
|---|---|---|---|
| Right to Deletion | Conditional | Yes (Article 17) | Yes |
| Key Exemptions | Financial & legal obligations | Legal obligations, public interest, legal claims | Completing transactions, fraud detection |
| Response Deadline | ~21 days (best practice) | 30 days | 45 days |
| Penalties | Regulatory enforcement | Up to €20M / 4% global revenue | Up to $7,500 per violation |
| Consent Requirements | Required for data processing | Lawful basis required | Opt-out of sale/sharing |

# Practical Implementation Challenges

Handling global deletion requests presents several operational challenges:

### Cross-Border Compliance Complexity

Different jurisdictions impose **different legal rights, exemptions, and response timelines**, requiring region-specific workflows.

### Data Mapping Challenges

Organizations must know **all locations where personal data is stored**, including:

- CRM systems
- Marketing platforms
- Payment processors
- Shipping providers
- Analytics systems

Without a **comprehensive data inventory**, compliance becomes difficult.

### Third-Party Processor Coordination

Regulations like GDPR require companies to notify **all third-party processors** holding the user's data.

Failure to do so can lead to **compliance gaps**.

### Data Retention Conflicts

Deletion rights may conflict with legal obligations such as:

- Tax record retention
- Fraud investigation
- Ongoing disputes

A **well-defined data retention policy** is required.
